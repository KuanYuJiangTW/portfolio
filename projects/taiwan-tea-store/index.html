The Challenge

Escaping the marketplace trap

The client — a family-run highland tea farm in Meishan, Chiayi — had been selling through third-party platforms for years. The problem: high commission fees, no control over customer data, and a brand experience that didn't reflect the quality of their product.

The goal was to migrate entirely to a self-owned platform: full control over the storefront, payments, and customer relationships — with zero reliance on external marketplaces.

The Hard Parts

Three problems most developers can't solve

1
ECPay Payment Integration

ECPay is Taiwan's dominant payment gateway, but its developer documentation is sparse and primarily in Chinese. Integrating credit card, ATM transfer, and convenience store payment codes required working through undocumented edge cases — particularly around webhook verification and order status callbacks. This is a skill most international developers simply don't have.

2
Triple OAuth Login

Taiwanese consumers expect to log in with LINE — not just Google or Facebook. Implementing all three OAuth providers simultaneously, with a unified user identity system backed by Supabase Auth, required careful session management to avoid account duplication across providers.

3
Order Management System

Beyond the storefront, the platform needed a full admin dashboard: order tracking, status updates, and automated transactional emails via Resend. Building this within Next.js API Routes — without a separate backend service — kept the architecture clean and the deployment simple.

The Result

A platform they fully own

The platform launched successfully and is now live at taiwantea.store. It replaced the client's dependency on third-party marketplaces entirely, giving them:

→ Full ownership of their storefront and customer data
→ Lower per-transaction costs vs. marketplace commissions
→ A brand experience that matches the quality of their product
→ A codebase they can maintain and expand independently
→ Infrastructure fully tested and ready to scale
